
# Utility Hydraulic System
## Overview
The Utility system provides pressure to non essential aircraft services such as:
* [Rudder]()             
* [Landing Gear]()       
* [Gear Doors]()         
* [Wheel Brakes]()       
* [Wing Flaps]()         
* [Nose Wheel Steering]()
* [Speed Brake]()   <!-- LINK TO FCS SPEED BRAKE -->     
* [Gun and Purge Doors]()
* [RAT Doors]()          

## Operation
### Speed Brake Switch
The speed brake switch is located on the throttle and commands the speed brake (aft) open or closed (forward).

### Nose Wheel Steering Button
The nose wheel steering button is located on the stick near the pinky finger. One time depression of the button turns on nose wheel steering and subsequent depression turns off nose wheel steering.

### Hydraulic Pressure Gauge
Select the "Utility" position on the hydraulic gauge selector switch to display the Utility hydraulic pressure. 

### Flap Switch
The flap switch is a three position guarded switch that commands the flaps to up, takeoff, or landing positions.

### Anti Skid Switch
The anti skid switch commands hydraulic pressure to the skid detectors.

### Emergency Flight Control System Power
The emergency hydaulic pump lever can be used to provide hydraulic power to the Utility system via extension of the [ram air turbine (RAT)]().

### Emergency Speed Brake Dump
The emergency speed brake dump lever can be used to release the speed brake hydraulic pressure.

### Emergency Flap Switch
The emergency flap switch is a covered two position switch that commands emergency flap accumulator pressure to extend the flaps.

### Landing Gear Handle
The landing gear position is commanded by the landing gear handle located on the front left console. The landing gear can be commanded up or down. See [gear warning tone](), see [gear position lights]().

### Landing Gear Emergency Lowering Handle
The landing gear emergency lowering handle releases the landing gear and landing gear door up locks. The main landing gear will free fall under gravity, and the nose gear will be hydraulically actuated via the nose gear emergency lowering accumulator.
